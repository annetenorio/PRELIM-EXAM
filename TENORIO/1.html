overflow

original poem by Anne Tenorio

i can’t express this undenied loss of motivation.
whether it may be a distraction or just no sense of direction.
I was on it, at first, I was completely on the game,
but in the long run i have no idea what made it foul and lost my name.

throughout the weekend, my bed just sucks me in.
I can’t seem to let go, I just give in.
but what in the enigma of this feelings begin
should I allow myself to go down or should I rise up again?

I don’t want to feel the pressure of constantly expecting something to happen,
sometimes it’s just better to make me feel stuffs rather than staying numb again.
I know it’s much harder to pretend it’s all okay,
sometimes I know I can find strength in all the delay.

Once I look forward now, I have no energy to meet.
For I am so excited to be and so excited to see.
This is what most of people around my age hate,
But it’s the only thing I’m good at and I appreciate.

Nonetheless, I don’t have the energy to see,
I just feel anxious this day would come and I am in nowhere near as ready.
Notes unfinished, tasks unsubmitted
How can I say I’m good at it, if I think I’m not committed?
